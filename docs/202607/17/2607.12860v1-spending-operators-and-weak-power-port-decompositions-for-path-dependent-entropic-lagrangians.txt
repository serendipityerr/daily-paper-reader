# Spending Operators and Weak Power-Port Decompositions for Path-Dependent Entropic Lagrangians 

Huilong Ren College of Civil Engineering, Tongji University, Shanghai, China 

hlren@tongji.edu.cn 

Abstract 

History-dependent entropic formulations require a precise meaning for channelwise terminal variation and a spatial power split that remains valid for weak diffusion fields. This paper establishes both structures and places them in a single Path-Dependent Entropic Lagrangian construction. Endpoint calibration and cocycle additivity uniquely determine the oriented spending increment, while a channelwise extension of a decomposed power port yields explicit terminal differentials for scalar dissipation and potential-weighted fluxes. The diffusion split is formulated at Sobolev, finite-energy distributional, and bounded-variation/divergence-measure regularity. A natural-dual-space counterexample proves that chemical-potential-weighted residuals do not imply conservation, and an independent multiplier restores the unweighted balance. These ingredients are assembled in one augmented thermo-diffusion PDEL functional on a synchronized product space. Its admissible directional stationarity produces energetic and thermal conjugacy, species balance, flux closure, entropy production, and the two terminal power-routing channels. The same construction admits a finite-energy weak form and specializes to the Cahn–Hilliard equation. 

Keywords: Path-Dependent Entropic Lagrangian; channelwise terminal variation; spending differential; diffusion power port; divergence-measure field; weak Leibniz rule; energy-dissipation principle; Cahn–Hilliard equation.

# 1 Introduction 

Variational descriptions of irreversible evolution have developed from several choices of primary structure. Onsager’s reciprocal relations and Biot’s irreversible thermodynamics express dissipative evolution through force–rate pairings and dissipation functions, while generalized standard materials and incremental constitutive principles encode material evolution by convex potentials (Onsager, 1931a; Onsager, 1931b; Biot, 1955; Gurtin, 1963; Halphen and Nguyen, 1975; Ortiz and Stainier, 1999; Doi, 2011). Global trajectory principles of Brezis–Ekeland type and later energy-dissipation functionals characterize complete dissipative paths once the energy and evolution graph have been specified (Brezis and Ekeland, 1976b; Brezis and Ekeland, 1976a; Ghoussoub and Tzou, 2004; Mielke and Ortiz, 2008; Mielke and Stefanelli, 2011). Generalized gradient systems and GENERIC formulations organize constitutive contact and reversible–irreversible coupling at the state-space level (Grmela and Öttinger, 1997; Öttinger and Grmela, 1997; Mielke et al., 2014; Liero et al., 2017; Mielke, 2023). Geometric and action-based approaches resolve complementary aspects of nonconservative dynamics. Stokes–Dirac and port-Hamiltonian systems represent bulk–boundary power ex-change through an interconnection structure (Schaft and Maschke, 2002; Le Gorrec et al., 1arXiv:2607.12860v1 [math-ph] 14 Jul 2026 2005; Schaft and Maschke, 2013). Herglotz and contact formulations modify the action or phase-space geometry, and doubled-variable actions impose physical identification after varia-tion (Georgieva et al., 2003; Bravetti et al., 2017; Galley, 2013). Constraint-based formulations introduce dissipative constraints or thermodynamic displacements (Fukagawa and Fujitani, 2012; Gay-Balmaz and Yoshimura, 2017a; Gay-Balmaz and Yoshimura, 2017b), whereas microforce balances and mixed rate potentials generate Cahn–Hilliard-type systems (Gurtin, 1996; Miehe et al., 2014). These theories solve different parts of the irreversible variational problem, but their defining structures do not determine a calibrated terminal extension of accumulated power together with independently tested weak conservation and a finite-energy diffusion-port product. The Path-Dependent Entropic Lagrangian (PDEL) framework was introduced for irreversible thermomechanical systems and subsequently developed as a path-dependent variational formu-lation of irreversible thermodynamics (Ren, 2025; Ren, 2026). It treats accumulated process power as a variational object and seeks to generate balance, thermodynamic conjugacy, and dissipative power routing from one history-dependent entropic construction. Those works establish the physical construction and representative field equations; the present paper isolates the mathematical structures required for terminal spending and weak diffusion ports. Three analytical obstructions must then be resolved. Synchronization fixes neither the amplitude nor the orientation of complementary terminal contributions. Pairing a conservation residual only with the realized chemical potential does not establish the residual itself. The classical product rule for a potential-weighted flux is not defined at the regularity of finite-energy diffusion fields. This paper resolves these obstructions. Endpoint calibration and cocycle additivity uniquely determine the oriented history increment, and the associated spending operator characterizes the compatible state–clock covectors. A channelwise extension gives explicit terminal differen-tials for scalar dissipation and for a decomposed diffusion port. The spatial decomposition is established at Sobolev, finite-energy distributional, and bounded-variation/divergence-measure regularity. A counterexample in the natural dual space excludes weighted conservation as a general argument; an independent multiplier yields the unweighted balance. These ingredients are assembled in a single thermo-diffusion PDEL functional on a synchronized product space, whose directional stationarity generates conjugacy, balance, constitutive closure, entropy production, and both terminal power-routing rules. The Cahn–Hilliard equation provides a finite-energy realization. The remainder of the paper is outlined as follows. Section 2 introduces rates, efforts, dissipation potentials, and the history-locked terminal derivative. Section 3 constructs the calibrated two-clock extension, its spending differential, and its localized channel form. Section 4 develops the diffusion power-port decomposition from smooth fields to finite-energy and divergence-measure settings. Sections 5 and 6 establish independently tested conservation, constitutive contact, boundary power, and energy-dissipation equivalence. Section 7 assembles these ingredients in one thermo-diffusion functional, and section 8 gives the Cahn–Hilliard realization. Section 9 summarizes the analytical contribution, scope, and implications for further path-dependent models.

# 4 Weak spatial power-port decomposition 

Let Ω ⊂ Rd be a bounded Lipschitz domain. The scalar potential is denoted by μ and the flux by j. The classical identity

4.1 Smooth geometric form 

Let Ω temporarily be an oriented Riemannian manifold with volume form vol , and let J := ιj vol 

be the (d − 1) -form associated with the flux. Then 

dJ = (div j)vol , dμ ∧ J = ( j · ∇ μ)vol . (4.2) The exterior-derivative Leibniz rule gives

# 7 A single PDEL functional for thermo-diffusion

# 9 Discussion and conclusion 

The analysis isolates four structures that must coexist in a history-dependent diffusion prin-ciple: a calibrated off-diagonal history extension, complementary channel directions for one accumulated power, conservation tested independently of the realized chemical potential, and a power-port decomposition defined at the regularity of weak solutions. None of these structures substitutes for another. Calibration fixes terminal orientation, the mixed multiplier gives the unweighted balance, the weak Leibniz theory defines the spatial channels, and convex constitutive contact supplies the dissipation sign. The spatial theory covers three levels of regularity. At H(div ) regularity, the internal components close through the normal-trace boundary power. At finite energy, the balance component is the distribution μ ⋄ div j. At BV –DM ∞ regularity, the force–flux component becomes a Radon measure and retains interfacial power. These extensions distinguish the result from smooth channel bookkeeping and permit direct use in weak diffusion formulations. The single thermo-diffusion functional shows that terminal spending, energetic and ther-mal conjugacy, unweighted species balance, diffusion and heat-flux closure, and the entropy equation arise from one synchronized product-space stationarity structure. The Cahn–Hilliard realization confirms the construction in the natural finite-energy setting. Relations to es-tablished variational structures have been stated where the corresponding ingredients enter: convex dissipation in section 2, duplicated variables in section 3, geometric boundary power in section 4, mixed Onsager structure in section 5, and thermodynamic constraints in section 7. The terminal derivative proved here is history-locked: accumulated interior values remain fixed while terminal channel coordinates vary before synchronization. Constitutive memory with vertical path perturbations requires an enlarged path space, and weak nonconvex energies retain an explicit chain-rule hypothesis. Within this scope, the scalar spending rule and the diffusion-port channel rule are established independently of a particular application and can be invoked directly in further thermomechanical, reactive, and multiphysics entropic Lagrangians.
