# Abstract 

Generative models have shown impressive results in speech enhancement but often suffer from multi-step inference. We propose SB-RF, a one-step generative framework integrat-ing Rectified Flow (RF) with Schr¨ odinger Bridge (SB) the-ory. SB-RF constructs a conditional bridge between clean and noisy speech distributions via entropy-regularized optimal transport. By aligning SB trajectories with the optimal trans-port geodesic through the velocity-matching objective of RF, SB-RF enables high-quality enhancement with one-step gen-eration. Experiments demonstrate that SB-RF achieves lead-ing performance among generative methods on the VoiceBank-DEMAND benchmark. Furthermore, to fully assess perfor-mance in challenging real-world scenarios, we evaluate SB-RF on a simulated low signal-to-noise ratio test set using an ex-panded training dataset. Under these conditions, SB-RF ex-hibits strong and competitive robustness with high efficiency, validating its potential for real-world applications. 

Index Terms : speech enhancement, diffusion model, one-step generation, Schr¨ oinger bridge, rectified flow 

# 1. Introduction 

Speech enhancement (SE) aims to recover clean speech from noisy observations, serving as a fundamental preprocessing stage for both human auditory perception (e.g., telecommuni-cations, hearing aids) and downstream tasks (e.g., automatic speech recognition, speaker verification). Classical signal pro-cessing methods such as spectral subtraction and Wiener filter-ing are computationally efficient but often struggle in challeng-ing scenarios. Data-driven approaches include discriminative models that learn a direct mapping from noisy to clean speech signals [1, 2, 3], as well as generative models that learn the clean speech distribution and reconstruct the clean target conditioned on the noisy observation [4, 5, 6, 7]. Although diffusion-based generative models have achieved remarkable performance, their slow inference resulting from the large number of function evaluations (NFE) has been widely criticized. Standard Diffusion Probabilistic Models such as CD-iffuSE [8] formulate SE as an iterative reversal of a stochastic process, requiring 50-200 NFEs. Continuous-time stochastic differential equation (SDE) approaches, such as SGMSE+ [9] and BBED [10], reduce NFEs to 10-30. To further acceler-ate inference speed, Flow Matching (FM) and Rectified Flow (RF) learn a deterministic velocity vector defined via an ordi-nary differential equation (ODE), achieving competitive results with about 5 NFEs [11]. Recently, one-step FM methods have emerged [12, 13, 14, 15]. Among these, MeanFlowSE [16] stands out as a strong recent approach. However, its perfor-mance relies heavily on large-scale pretrained encoders (e.g., WavLM-Large), leading to significantly higher computational and memory costs that limit on-device deployment. On the other hand, standard RF constructs a straight-line interpolation path between clean and noisy speech. We argue that such a rigid deterministic constraint forces the model to learn a sin-gle trajectory, which is suboptimal for the highly stochastic and multimodal SE task. In this work, we propose SB-RF, a standalone, high-fidelity one-step generative framework that couples Schr¨ odinger Bridge (SB) theory with RF. Theoretically, standard RF can be viewed as a degenerate Brownian Bridge with zero diffusion coeffi-cient. Motivated by this isomorphism, we reintroduce stochas-ticity during training. This effectively relaxes the rigid point-to-point constraint into a robust ”probability tube”, smoothing the learned velocity vector and improving robustness. How-ever, heuristic noise injection alone is insufficient for precise convergence to the target distribution. We therefore ground the framework in SB theory, treating the generative process as an entropy-regularized optimal transport problem. The result-ing SB yields data-adaptive time marginals and geometry-aware trajectories for the perturbed signal xt, enhancing stability and sample quality. Crucially, in the vanishing regularization limit, SB converges to the optimal transport geodesic, resulting in a constant-velocity trajectory. To approximate these dynamics, we leverage the velocity-matching objective of RF, enabling high-fidelity speech reconstruction in a single step without re-lying on external pretrained models. While VoiceBank-DEMAND (VB-DMD) is widely used, its limited scale and diversity may not fully reflect real-world performance. We therefore evaluate the proposed methods un-der two settings: (1) the standard VB-DMD dataset, and (2) an expanded setting with richer training data and challenging low-SNR test sets to assess robustness and generalization. Ex-periments demonstrate that SB-RF achieves competitive perfor-mance on the standard benchmark and outperforms baselines in challenging low-SNR conditions with NFE = 1, marking a sig-nificant step toward the practical deployment of one-step gener-ative SE.

# 3. Proposed method 

In this section, we propose SB-RF, a stochastic extension of RF that integrates the optimal transport principles of the SB. While standard RF relies on deterministic interpolation, our method formulates speech enhancement as an entropic optimal trans-port problem. By combining the geometry-aware prior of SB with the straight-line path enforcing objective of RF, SB-RF preserves the high-efficiency inference (NFE = 1) while signif-icantly enhancing robustness against discretization errors and complex noisy conditions. 

3.1. Brownian Bridge-based Rectified Flow 

Standard RF learns a deterministic ODE trajectory to connect distributions [11]. However, this deterministic assumption of-ten limits flexibility in capturing the complex, multimodal pos-terior distributions of clean speech. To introduce stochasticity for robust generation, we first analyze the relationship between the Brownian Bridge (BB) solution and RF. and the target drift y − x, which is consistent with (4). 3.2. Schr¨ odinger Bridge-based Rectified Flow 

While introducing BB-based stochasticity to RF improves ro-bustness, it fundamentally relies on the linear mean trajectory defined in (9). However, speech signals inherently exhibit di-versity and reside on a complex, nonlinear manifold. The relationship between observed noisy spectrograms and their corresponding clean targets is highly dynamic and context-dependent, varying greatly across diverse signal-to-noise ratios (SNRs) and noise types. Consequently, a simple linear interpo-lation for xt fails to capture these complicated, data-dependent dependencies. This geometric mismatch makes the network learn an inefficient transport path, imposing a heavy burden on the model capacity to compensate for the errors arising from such rigid assumptions. To overcome this, we propose SB-RF, which replaces the fixed linear prior with a learnable, geometry-aware trajectory formulated via SB. Specifically, we replace the BB kernel with the SB process defined in (7) to generate the perturbed signal 

xt during training. By minimizing the KL divergence to a cho-sen reference process under endpoint constraints connecting the noisy distribution πy and the clean distribution πx, SB naturally discovers the most probable transport path that respects the in-trinsic manifold of the speech data. Consequently, the result-ing mean and variance schedules for xt are dynamically gov-erned by the Schr¨ odinger potentials, enabling the SB solution to adaptively model the complex dependencies between noisy and clean speech. Standard SB paths are inherently stochastic and curved due to the diffusion term arising from entropy regularization. How-ever, in the limit of vanishing regularization, the entropic in-terpolation converges to the optimal transport geodesic. Cru-cially, under quadratic Euclidean cost, these optimal transport geodesics are deterministic straight lines traversed at constant speed [22, 23]. To align with this theoretical optimum, we adopt the velocity-matching objective of RF. The learned vec-tor vθ (xt, t ) is trained to match the linear target y − x, thereby approximating constant-velocity dynamics along the transport path. Our method effectively reduces the local truncation error of the Euler solver, facilitating high-quality inference even with one-step generation.

# 4. Experiments 

4.1. Datasets and Evaluation Metrics 

4.1.1. Datasets 

To comprehensively evaluate both standard denoising perfor-mance and generative robustness, we organize our experiments into two tracks. Track A follows the standard VB-DMD bench-mark for comparison with prior work. Track B focuses on the generative capacity under complex real-world conditions. This track introduces an expanded training set, since limited datasets may constrain the inherent potential of generative methods in complex environments. Furthermore, we generate a simulated low-SNR test set, as the robustness of denoising under such con-ditions remains a critical challenge for real-world applications. • Standard setting (VB-DMD) The VB-DMD training set comprises 11,572 utterances, generated by mixing clean speech from the VCTK dataset [25] with DEMAND noise signals [26] and two artificial noises (babble and speech-shaped) at SNRs of 0, 5, 10, 15 dB. The test set con-tains 824 utterances with SNRs of 2.5, 7.5, 12.5, 17.5 dB. • Low-SNR robustness setting: For training, we randomly sampled 1000 hours of clean speech from WenetSpeech4TTS (Premium subset) [27] and DNS-4 [28] (500 hours each). The noise set includes DNS-4 and MUSAN [29]. Mixtures are generated on-the-fly with SNRs uniformly sampled from -10 to 15 dB. For evaluation, we randomly selected 10,000 utterances of clean speech from AISHELL-1 [30] and Lib-riSpeech [31], mixed with noise clips from WHAM! [32] at SNRs uniformly sampled from -10 to 0 dB. Crucially, we strictly ensure no overlap in speakers, utterances or noise clips between the training and test sets. 

4.1.2. Evaluation Metrics 

The performance is evaluated in terms of perceptual evaluation of speech quality (PESQ) [33] for perceived quality, extended short-term objective intelligibility (ESTOI) [34] for intelligibil-ity, scale-invariant measures SI-SDR, SI-SIR, and SI-SAR [35] and non-intrusive measures via DNSMOS P.808 [36]. We also report the NFE to assess efficiency. 

4.2. Implementation Details Experimental Setup. All speech signals are resampled to 16 kHz, and then transformed into the complex STFT representa-tion with a window size of 510 and a hop size of 128. We obtain 

F = 256 frequency bins and concatenate K = 256 consecu-tive frames to form the model input X ∈ RF ×K . An amplitude transformation ˜c = β |c|α ej∠c is applied to compensate for the heavy-tailed distribution of all complex STFT coefficients c

with α = 0 .5 and β = 0 .33 [37, 38]. For the bridge process, the time boundaries are set to ϵ = 0 .03 and T = 0 .97 .We adopt NCSN++ as the backbone with 65.6M parameters and 265 GMACs [9]. The weighting hyperparameters are set to 

λ1 = 33 and λ2 = 3 , and the multi-scale Mel-spectrogram loss follows the configuration in [24]. We use the Adam optimizer with an initial learning rate of 0.0001 and an exponential de-cay of 0.999 per epoch. The batch size is set to 16 per GPU. All models are trained from scratch with random initialization. Training is conducted on 8 NVIDIA RTX A800 GPUs. 

Baseline Systems. The proposed SB-RF is compared against representative baselines. MP-SENet [2] is a representative discriminative method. SGMSE+ [9] and BBED [10] are score-based generative methods. SB-VE [19] is a genera-tive method that directly predicts the clean signal based on SB theory. CFM [39], LARF [11] and COSE [15] belong to velocity-matching generative methods, where LARF (modified RF) and COSE (MeanFlow) are representative one-step gener-ation benchmarks. 

Evaluation Setup. For Track A, the proposed methods are trained and evaluated solely on VB-DMD, and all baselines adopt official results from the original paper or author-released checkpoints are used when available. As for Track B, we retrain selected baselines and the proposed methods on the expanded training set. All baselines are implemented with officially re-leased code and recommended configurations. 

4.3. Experimental Results

With one-step generation, the pro-posed SB-RF achieves leading performance among generative SE approaches, recording the highest PESQ of 3.39, SI-SDR of 19.5 dB, and the best ESTOI of 0.88. It significantly out-performs conventional diffusion baselines such as SGMSE+, BBED and SB-VE, which suffer from high inference latency (15 to 60 NFEs). When compared to the conditional flow matching (CFM) method designed for fast inference (typically requiring 5 NFEs), SB-RF yields significantly higher PESQ and SI-SDR with NFE = 1. Furthermore, SB-RF outperforms one-step generative baselines such as LARF and COSE by a large margin (e.g., +0.37 PESQ over COSE), verifying that the pro-posed integration of the SB prior with the RF objective provides superior performance with high efficiency. # 5. Conclusion 

In this paper, we present SB-RF, an SB-guided RF frame-work that leverages the RF velocity-matching objective to straighten SB trajectories toward constant-velocity optimal transport geodesics, enabling high-fidelity speech reconstruc-tion with one-step generation. Experimental results demon-strate that SB-RF not only delivers leading performance on the standard VB-DMD benchmark among generative methods, but also maintains strong and competitive robustness under chal-lenging low-SNR conditions even with only one-step sampling (NFE = 1).
